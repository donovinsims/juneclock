## Competitive Tiers

### Direct Competitors
*Same solution category, same problem — other automation consultants and local marketing agencies selling AI / automation services to local service businesses.*

These competitors offer similar "done-for-you automation" packages, often bundled into monthly retainers. Many are marketing agencies that have added AI front desk / chatbot services as an offering.

**How they fall short:**
- **Monthly pricing:** $500–$5,000/month, locked into annual contracts. Clockout is $497 one-time.
- **Rented system:** The automation lives on their platform. If they go under, your system dies. Clockout hands over every credential.
- **Generic templates:** Agency "AI solutions" are often pre-built chatbots bolted on with minimal customization. Clockout maps the actual workflow first.
- **Quick-fix chatbot trap:** Most "AI front desk" products are simple chatbots that answer FAQs — they don't follow up quotes, chase reviews, or re-engage past customers. Leads still leak. Clockout closes the full loop.
- **Slow setup:** Agencies quote 2–6 weeks for setup. Clockout delivers in 7 days.
- **Login proliferation:** Yet another dashboard for the owner to check. Clockout installs inside existing tools.
- **High churn incentive:** Agencies profit when you stay on the retainer. Clockout profits when the build works and you're done.
- **67% failure rate:** Industry research shows 67% of small business automation projects fail or deliver minimal results — because they slap on a tool without mapping the workflow first.

### Secondary Competitors
*Different solution, same problem — field service software and CRMs that claim to solve these issues.*

- **ServiceTitan:** The 800-lb gorilla. Dominant in HVAC/Plumbing/Electrical. Great dispatch and scheduling. But:
  - **$5K–$50K** implementation cost before you get a working system
  - **$39K–$46K** average early termination fee — switching is financially crippling
  - **Auto-renewal traps:** 30-day auto-renew clauses, requires certified letter to cancel
  - **Hidden pricing:** Implementation fees, training fees, data migration fees, custom integration fees
  - **G2/Capterra scores:** 3.8/5 on G2 (242 reviews) with complaints about hidden fees, support quality, and lock-in; 4.0/5 on Capterra
  - **BBB complaints:** Multiple complaints about contract terms, billing disputes, and inability to cancel
  - **Doesn't fix the core problem:** Still requires a human to answer calls, follow up quotes, and chase reviews. The software doesn't do the work — it just tracks it.
  - **Clockout advantage:** Clockout works inside ServiceTitan (and every other tool) to close the gaps the software leaves open. No migration. No ETF. No lock-in.
- **Housecall Pro, Jobber:** Lighter-weight alternatives to ServiceTitan. Better UX. More affordable. But same core limitation — they track work, they don't do the work. Still need a human for lead response, follow-up, and review generation.
- **Property Management software (AppFolio, Buildium, Yardi):** Handle rent collection, maintenance requests, lease tracking. But don't automate lead follow-up for vacant units, tenant renewal campaigns, or review generation. Leasing agents still manually chase prospects — same follow-up gap as trades.
- **Real Estate CRMs (kvCORE, Follow Up Boss, BoomTown):** Pipeline tracking for agents. Powerful reporting. But require the agent to actually make the calls and send the messages. Adoption is low — the tools are only as good as the person using them.
- **GoHighLevel (GHL):** Powerful marketing/automation platform, but requires configuration and ongoing management. Most trade shop owners don't have time to set it up properly. Ends up as an expensive abandoned tool.
- **Kajabi / ClickFunnels:** Not trade-specific. Requires significant DIY effort. No phone/lead response capability.

**How they fall short:**
- All require ongoing configuration and management
- No one builds the system for you
- The owner still has to figure out what to automate and how
- Most don't handle phone calls (the #1 leak point)

### Indirect Competitors
*Conflicting approach to the same problem.*

- **Hiring a virtual assistant:** Cheaper upfront ($400–$800/month overseas) but:
  - **High turnover:** VA quits or goes quiet. Owner starts over. 67% automation project failure rate includes the "hired a VA who left" pattern.
  - **Requires management:** Owner still has to train, supervise, and audit the VA — defeating the purpose.
  - **No system build:** When the VA leaves, the knowledge goes with them. Clockout leaves behind a documented, running system.
  - **Time zone / language friction:** Overseas VAs don't answer local evening calls — the #1 leak point.
- **DIY automation (Make, Zapier, n8n):** Powerful but requires technical skill and maintenance. Owner doesn't have the time. 67% of automation projects in small businesses fail — and most of those are DIY attempts.
- **Hiring a local dispatcher:** $40K–$55K/year + benefits + training. They quit in 90 days. Now the owner is back to square one, and the work is piling up even higher.
- **"The owner IS the system" trap:** The most common "solution" — owner just works harder. Answers calls at 10pm. Does data entry on Sundays. Never takes a vacation. Works until burnout or exit. This is the default state Clockout is solving.
- **Doing nothing:** The default. Owner accepts the leaks as "just how it is in this industry." Continues losing $5,400+/month to missed calls and unfollowed quotes while telling themselves they'll "fix it next season."

- Enterprise companies with dedicated ops teams
- Tech companies / SaaS businesses
- Owners who aren't willing to change any workflows
- Businesses outside the United States
- Anyone expecting a zero-effort plug-and-play solution (Clockout requires the owner to participate in the audit)
- Shops that already have a functioning end-to-end automation system
- Price-shoppers looking for the absolute cheapest option (Clockout is cheap for what it delivers, but not the cheapest possible solution)
- Near-retirement owners planning to sell within 1–2 years — ROI window is too short
- Owners who insist "my business is too unique" for any system — operational resistance that prevents implementation
- Owners who already tried and failed and are now fully cynical about automation — need the audit to prove the case, but may still be a no-go
